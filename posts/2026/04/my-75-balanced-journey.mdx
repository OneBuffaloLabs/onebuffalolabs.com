When I launched **Grit 2.0**, I wanted to prove that discipline doesn't have to be "one size fits all". To put the new customization engine to the test, I officially started my own **75 Balanced** journey on February 5th.

## Why 75 Balanced?

I am a big fan of the 75 Hard concept, but living in **Buffalo, NY**, the "outdoor workout" rule is a dealbreaker in February. Blistering winters make that requirement nearly impossible some days.

I chose the **75 Balanced** path because it focuses on "Sustainable Consistency." It's designed for people like me‚Äîparents and business owners who need a program that fits a real 9-to-5 schedule without the high risk of burnout.

## The Challenge Within the Challenge

Beyond the workouts and diet, there is a much bigger factor at play in this journey: **time management**.

I‚Äôm currently navigating this challenge while working a full-time software engineering job and simultaneously building my own software company, **One Buffalo Labs**. On top of that professional load, our home life is about to get a lot busier. My wife, Hillary, is due with our second son this March.

Starting this 75-day challenge now means I‚Äôll be balancing the "newborn phase" right alongside my 8-year-old son, Leo. Figuring out how to stay consistent with my workouts and nutrition while dealing with 3 AM feedings and the demands of two separate software ventures will be the ultimate test of the system I built.

## The Rules of the Road

For the next 75 days, my daily checklist looks like this:

- **Diet:** Follow a specific plan with one cheat meal per week.
- **No Alcohol:** I am choosing to cut alcohol completely for the duration of this challenge.
- **Workouts:** One 45-minute workout plus a 15-minute active recovery session (like a stretch or a walk) every day.
- **Water:** Drink 3 Liters (~100 oz) of water daily.
- **Reading:** Read 10 pages of any book.
- **Photos:** Take a progress picture once a week.

### Accountability: The Penalty Day System

One feature I built specifically for the Balanced protocol is the **Penalty Day** system. If you miss a day, you add a day to the end of the challenge instead of restarting at Day 1.

I designed this to provide a **one-shot redemption**‚Äîa way to handle a legitimate life emergency without losing all momentum. However, it is a redemption tool, not a failsafe to keep failing and pushing your end date back indefinitely. Interestingly, I found that even with the arrival of our son and the dual-career grind, the system was ultimately unnecessary; the commitment to the routine kept me from ever needing it.
